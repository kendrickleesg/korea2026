Seongeup Folk VillagePyoseon, east Jeju
9:30 AM
A living, inhabited village continuously occupied for around 600 years — one of Jeju's most authentic cultural sites and a National Folklore Cultural Heritage. Walk the narrow lava stone-walled alleys (olle), see thatched-roof hanoks still lived in by residents, and explore the ancient fortress walls, century-old zelkova trees, and Dolhareubang stone statues. Free entry. Allow 1.5 hours. Grandma-friendly — paths are paved and wide throughout.
CultureKids
Tip: Some locals may offer guided tours then expect you to purchase herbal products — politely decline if not interested. Head for the fortress inner village rather than the outer tourist stalls. Kids can try on hanbok and archery at activity spots.
Allergy: If purchasing any traditional snacks or sweets at village stalls, read packaging carefully and confirm no peanut content before the child eats. Traditional Jeju confections sometimes include peanut-based fillings — verify before buying.
